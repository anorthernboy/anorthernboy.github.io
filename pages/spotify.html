Overview

Spotify is a fantastic example of a modern, disruptive innovator - a company with clear values and a brand personality which is appropriate for it's target customer, transforming the music industry wholesale through better understanding of user motivations. Through identifying a move towards the idea of a sharing economy [access over ownership] Spotify was able to build and deliver a compelling value proposition to the market.

As a technology solution, Spotify demonstrates fantastic understanding of modern user emotions when consuming music - addressing choice overload through carefully presented recommendations and the continuous playlist feature which delivers similar content indefinitely without further user input. This content unbundling and delivery technique is supported by the strategic measurement of positive intentional behaviours to build an in depth user profile - creating a positive feedback loop in building accurate user personas.

The Spotify application is consistent across all platforms displaying excellent user-centred design patterns and adhering to usability standards. Spotify is known to conduct extensive AB testing across platforms and has built internal tools to make test data accessible to Product Managers. Big data is key to the success of Spotify, but through understanding user concerns they are able to pre-empt user privacy concerns by consciously sharing data playfully through features Wrapped which animates the user listening habits across the previous year. While this doesn't mean Spotify is not the main beneficiary from the data collected, to some extent they are able to share this value with users.

While Spotify is executing very successfully against a product roadmap, there remains opportunity to grow significant revenue [premium subscribers] through specific customer profiling. My hypothesis posits there exists a feedback loop within the recommendation engine which creates barriers for independent artists.

There is an emerging movement by these smaller artists, appealing to authentic music fans to stop using the platform. The risk presented is also an opportunity for Spotify to engage these users and artists with new features.

I also believe that the popularity weighting within the recommendation engine results in a failure to handle artist complexity and disappoints some users.

Tomorrow Never Knows is not The Long and Winding Road after all.

The recent consumer movement toward analogue music [massive increase in vinyl sales, for example] suggests that a cohort of music consumers are looking for a more engaged experience in music discovery.

Problem

Using the Spotify Web API, I performed proof-of-hypothsis analysis using a playlist curated by me [shoegaze], a Spotify playlist from a relatively unknown artist in the genre [lightfoils], and a Spotify playlist from a commercial artist in the genre [ride]. Both artists feature on my curated playlist.

Initially, I plotted the popularity metrics for every artist included in each playlist to create an average measure of popularity across the playlist. The playlist curated by me [shoegaze], scored significantly lower on each metric of popularity and had a much more recent release date.

The playlist built by Spotify using the most popular artist according to their metrics [ride] demonstrated a massive amount of popularity bias and a more distant release date - which would theoretically suggest more time allows the artist to build popularity and presents another barrier to entry for new artists.

To investigate the similarities in musicality across each playlist, I employed the statistical analysis technique k-means clustering to group each track from the playlist based on sonic qualities as provided by the Spotify Web API.

Although not statistically significant, the results suggest my curated playlist [shoegaze] contained only three clusters, whereas the Spotify playlists demonstrated six different sonic clusters - the tracks included show much more variation in musicality.

Further investigation of sonic qualities across the three playlists shows the consistency of sound across my curated playlist more clearly. Although further investigation is required, the data suggests that a popular artist on Spotify will engage a wide variety of users and will be played/playlisted in many different circumstances.

A popular artist with a varied back catalogue - like The Beatles - might be included in a playlist of ballads as well as one of psychedelic rock and these very different actions can result in recommendations with significant sonic variation.

It should be noted that the desire for a consistent sound within a playlist is not a universal requirement.

User Persona

In order to offer a recommendation on these findings I began with a user profile which was built around Spotify's strategic objective to engage more older users. Only 11% of Spotify users are aged 45-54 year old which means there is less user data and therefore less relevant recommendations. By engaging an initial cohort of older users, the product begins to benefit from a network effect whereby preferences are revealed through actions.

Solution

During ideation I engaged in divergent thinking in order to explore solutions including incentivising crowdsourced musicality ratings, a viral engagement tool to encourage users to drag and drop loops from their favourite tracks and build something new [thus revealing underlying musical preferences], and the ability to scan a physical record collection to align Spotify profile with the analogue world.

After considering the pros and cons of various features, weighing up estimated effort against potential customer delight - while maintaining consistency of experience for core users - I proposed the implementation of optionally tweaking the recommendation engine while engaged in a playlist.

A filter tab is added to the bottom of the application, which contains three dials representing the weighting of each characteristic to feed into the recommendation algorithm - play me more tracks that sound like this and less artists closely associated with this track. The track queue will update in real time as the user sets preferences.
