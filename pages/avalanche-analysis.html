Avalanche Insights ‘human-in-the-loop’ analysis tool redesign

Complete end-to-end overhaul of a complex product flow enabling users to extract nuanced insights from open-ended text data

Overview

Avalanche Insights, a consultancy specialising in progressive politics, sought to scale their services by developing a product that could extract key themes and insights from open-ended text data. They needed a redesigned user flow that could allow non-expert users to easily navigate the complex human-in-the-loop machine learning process. With this goal in mind, I was recruited to facilitate a design sprint and create a product that would deliver a valuable data exploration and insights experience for users.


Goals

- Redesign user experience for complex 'human-in-the-loop' machine learning text analysis
- Create an intuitive experience for a non-expert user


Role

- Design Sprint Facilitator
- UI/UX Designer


Tools

Research and Discovery

The project followed the design sprint framework, with a timeframe of 2-3 weeks due to additional commitments across the team. In the understand phase, we conducted a UX audit of the incumbent solution and user interviews to identify the biggest barriers to users achieving their goals. Many challenges were identified, including a disconnect between user expectations and actual application behaviour, an inflexible flow limiting user actions to a linear process, and users unable to access the information they required in the interface.

To ensure solutions were grounded in feasibility, we conducted workshops with our data science lead, engineering manager, and Avalanche Insights analysts. This allowed for creative thinking and sourced ideas for utilising proprietary data science. Through this process, we identified opportunities to meet user needs and developed a problem statement: How can we use semantic diversity cluster analysis, natural language processing, and classification models to create an accessible, usable, and feasible product flow that allows non-expert users to extract themes from open-ended text data?

Journey Map

Extensive discovery interviews allowed me to develop a journey map with a number of stages that were constantly discussed by users. Initially (collect) the users were excited to start a new product, and reported a general contentment with their survey fielding software.

The very next stage (analyse) provided the biggest frustration, this was the realisation - often described as overwhelming - that the user would be required to sift through all survey responses to tease out themes and insights. It was typically reported that, even though the survey fielding tools included features to address this stage, their functionality was insufficient for the user requirements.

The third stage (curate) included pulling together visualisations, key quotes, and highlights to create presentation decks to report back to the organisation. Although this didn't cause the same dread as the previous stage, there were still some challenges around exploring data visualisations and crafting relevant stories.

The final stage for the user (share) is the final handoff of the recommendations and findings. At this stage the user reported feeling relieved having come through the previous two stages.


Design and Prototyping

Sketching was conducted asynchronously, and Crazy 8’s method was used for divergent thinking. We held a session to share Crazy 8’s ideas and then asked each participant to work on a solution sketch, an end-to-end proposal to address the problem statement. A decider was enlisted to review the solution sketches and advise on the most promising idea to move into the testing phase.

My proposal was selected, and I gradually increased the fidelity from sketch to wireframe, then higher fidelity screens, and finally an interactive prototype for testing. I collaborated closely with the Product Manager and drew on our shared knowledge of non-expert users gained over the course of numerous discovery interviews. Each data science concept was separated in the product flow, allowing for prioritisation of optimization if it proved valuable to the user.


Screens

Given the requirements set at the outset - broadly, use this data science approach and include these micro-interactions - it became very apparent that there would be real value in conducting a rumble whereby my proposal would be tested directly against the existing solution, following the same interview script, tasks, and success metrics.

A number of key features are presented in the following user flows:

Start Analysis

Upon completing their upload to the platform, users are presented with an empty state on the analysis dashboard. To start the analysis process, a clear call-to-action guides them to begin the machine-assisted analysis.

To alleviate users' feelings of overwhelm and confusion when starting from scratch, I implemented a cluster analysis feature that generates smart themes, which can be fully customised. This was a key value proposition identified through user interviews, where many users expressed a desire for a headstart on their analysis.

In the previous solution, users were required to manually confirm every AI suggestion, leading to confusion and a tedious user experience. The new solution automatically incorporates the AI suggestions into the analysis process, making it a breeze to manage them.

By streamlining the integration of AI into the analysis process, I made it easier for users to focus on the insights and actions that matter most. The result is a more efficient and user-friendly platform that empowers users to make data-driven decisions with confidence.



Explore Smart Themes

The user interface of the platform provides a comprehensive and unified view of all the analysis-related features. Users can easily navigate through themes and responses, and employ the search, sort, and filter functions to explore their data. The platform allows users to move fluidly in the direction their analysis takes them, without any restrictions on their path.

In the UX audit of the incumbent solution, users reported feeling limited in the direction they could take with their analysis. However, with the new interface, users can freely explore their data and draw insights in a way that best suits their needs.



Add a Response to a Theme

The micro-interaction that defines the completion of the analysis is associating responses with themes. To achieve this, my solution implements an interaction pattern commonly used in email inbox management. When a user selects a response by clicking on it, an action menu appears, offering various options.

Additionally, the user can simply drag and drop responses onto a theme to associate them. This interaction design was informed by industry best practices and user feedback, resulting in a simple and intuitive way to perform the essential task of associating responses with themes.



Add Multiple Responses to a New Theme

In my solution, the user can easily select multiple responses with a simple click and drag interaction. The action menu is still accessible for these selected responses, allowing the user to easily associate them with a theme or perform other actions. This eliminates the need for the cumbersome and unintuitive shift key multi-select method that was identified as a pain point in the UX audit.

Additionally, the "Create new theme" button provides an intuitive and streamlined way for users to create themes and organise their responses.



See Similar Responses

During the ideation process, I developed a novel use of existing data science called "See Similar Responses." This feature plots all responses on a vector map during cluster analysis and allows the user to select a reference point, exploring the closest responses from the map.

By enabling the user to surface and analyse multiple similar responses, their analysis process becomes much more efficient. This feature was designed with the user in mind, with the aim of improving their overall experience on the platform.



Organise Themes

Organising themes and creating theme hierarchies was a top requirement for almost all users. In the UX audit, this feature was identified as a major weakness for the incumbent solution as users were restricted in completing this step either at the end of the process or by navigating to a new tab.

In my solution, I embedded the ability to organise themes at any point in the analysis, right in the interface where the user is working. I recognised that organising themes is an iterative process and theme hierarchies are fluid, so easy access to this feature is crucial for the user.



Train Theme

A crucial feature of the platform is the ability for the user to train a theme, which provides direct feedback to the AI classification model and enables auto-tagging, significantly improving the efficiency of the analysis process.

However, the incumbent solution's interface for this feature was overly complex and confusing, as identified in user interviews. In my solution, I simplified the interface by incorporating a thumbs-up, thumbs-down interaction that users found intuitive and user-friendly. Additionally, I included a drawer in the interface where the user can add or remove responses added by both the user and the AI, providing further feedback to the classification model without disrupting the user's analysis flow.



Analysis Complete

In my solution, I recognised the importance of providing users with the flexibility to customise the depth and complexity of their analysis to fit their specific needs. Through user research and testing, I found that users valued the ability to tailor the level of detail in their analysis based on the specific use case or decision they were making.

Unlike the incumbent solution, my platform provides users with a range of analysis options that can be customised based on the level of complexity needed. This includes the ability to select and analyse only a subset of responses, or to perform a deep dive analysis of all available data. By providing this flexibility, users are able to complete their analysis more efficiently and with greater accuracy, resulting in better decision-making outcomes.



Outcome

In just two weeks, I created an end-to-end interactive prototype that outperformed the existing design solution, which had been in development for over a year. User testing measured task understanding, task completion, user delight, and willingness to share contact details and intention to use the product. My end-to-end solution was successful with all users, while the existing solution was seen as a work in progress and garnered no contact details.

Lessons Learned

Following the design sprint framework allowed for the creation of a successful end-to-end prototype in just two weeks. It was essential to collaborate closely with the Product Manager and draw on our shared knowledge of non-expert users. Separating each data science concept in the product flow allowed for prioritisation of optimization if it proved valuable to the user.

Conclusion

In conclusion, the redesign of the user experience for complex human-in-the-loop machine learning text analysis resulted in an intuitive experience for non-expert users. The end-to-end prototype created in the design sprint outperformed the existing solution and achieved strong user engagement. Through close collaboration and separating data science concepts in the product flow, we successfully met user needs and created a scalable product for Avalanche Insights.
