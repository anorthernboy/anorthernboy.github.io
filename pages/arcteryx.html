Arc'teryx product comparison feature development project

Product appraisal and ideation on the future development of an ecommerce feature from Arc'teryx utilising Generative AI

Overview

Arc'teryx is a well-known brand that specialises in outdoor equipment and clothing, and is highly regarded for its high-performance and technically advanced products, particularly in the realm of outdoor apparel, outerwear, and equipment for activities like hiking, climbing, skiing, and mountaineering.

Arc'teryx places a strong emphasis on innovation and quality in its designs, incorporating cutting-edge materials and technologies to create durable and functional products. The brand is known for its attention to detail, precision in manufacturing, and commitment to producing products that withstand challenging outdoor conditions.

Arc’teryx’ ecommerce website hosts a wonderfully innovative feature which allows users to compare up to three products. The implementation of this feature is functional, usable, accessible, making use of a drawer at the bottom of the screen which can be quickly accessed or hidden. This feature allows the user to explore multiple products without navigating away from the page they are browsing. This feature is valuable in somewhat recreating a brick-and-mortar type retail experience where a customer will typically select multiple products to compare.

This feature has also been designed with the user in mind: a typical Arc’teryx customer is likely to be an avid outdoor enthusiast who values performance and functionality, and appreciates Arc'teryx’ commitment to using high-quality materials and employing advanced manufacturing techniques to deliver products that are built to last. They also recognise the meticulous attention to detail and have a good understanding of the technical aspects of outdoor gear. In short, they are informed and investigate in depth before making their purchase decision.



Feature Appraisal

The Compare Products feature delivers both human-centred design and commercial ecommerce practices. It is presented in a thoughtful interface which is consistent across the ecommerce site and suggests consistent use of a strong foundational design system. The clean aesthetic reduces friction and optimises the product comparison experience by enabling the user to concentrate on the features without distraction. Further successes of the feature are outlined below:

Primary CTA

The primary call-to-action (CTA) is taking the user to the product page, and ultimately further along the user journey map toward the ultimate goal of completing a purchase. The CTA is also sticky, so the user always has access to this even when scrolling through the comparison.



Content Alignment

All products display the same section headers (e.g. Full Product Features) and - importantly - the same subsection headers (e.g. Collar Configuration) even when some products within the comparison are returning an empty state for the subsection. This ensures comparable information is always directly inline horizontally across the comparison.



Hover Interaction

There is a really useful hover interaction which subtly highlights the subsection row for all products, focusing the users attention on the key information.



Collapsible Content

Each main section is also collapsible, again allowing the user to quickly focus on their own comparison requirements - whether that be general product information, key features, or the materials and garment care instructions.



Problem

I believe the solution addresses the user story below:

As an Arc’teryx ecommerce customer,
I want to compare products,
So that I can choose the right product features for my needs.


However, if we were to apply a jobs-to-be-done (JTBD) frame of reference to this story, we might infer that the product comparison is actually a means to an end. In this instance, the job - what an individual really seeks to accomplish in a given circumstance - is only to choose the right product features for their individual needs.

We live in an age of multiple brand touchpoints, successful retail companies are omnichannel, which means combining the magic of in-store experiences, and the convenience and availability of online shopping. If we were to map the product comparison process onto the in-store experience, we might expect the user to select some products before seeking product expertise from retail staff. The retail staff perform the actual comparison, potentially asking further questions and suggesting alternative products as well.

Having identified the Arc’teryx customer as having “a good understanding of the technical aspects of outdoor gear, being informed and investigating in depth before making their purchase decision” this is not an absolute distinction to make - some customers will be attached to the comparison and self-discovery.

However, for Arc’teryx, the product experience is enhanced by highly-trained customer service staff with expert knowledge on all technical aspects of the product. This can be evidenced in the job description for a Product Training Designer at Arc’teryx:

As the Product Training Designer, you are responsible for the successful design and creation of Product Training experiences that drive passion and knowledge across all Arc’teryx commercial and Wholesale channels. Specifically, you will focus on the build-out and continual improvement of our product training experiences ranging from eLearning to facilitator-led Product Education training events. You will collaborate with cross-functional partners to create transformational learning experiences related to any of our current and future products. Using your innovative mindset, you will assess ways to make our Product Training content uniquely Arc’teryx. You are connected to our product teams and bring the voice of our designers and highly technical concepts to our commercial teams and wholesale partners.


From this we can develop a problem statement which aims to bring the Compare Products ecommerce feature closer to the in-store experience - we need to insert some expertise:

How might we provide a level of product expertise and advice,
To ecommerce customers using the Compare Products feature,
That is comparable to the in-store experience?*
