The GoodBox Co transactions reporting suite

Introducing data-driven, commercial-minded, decision-making into a rapidly scaling startup

Overview

In 2019, I joined The GoodBox Co as a Frontend Developer / UI Designer, a tech-for-good startup in the FinTech space that was experiencing massive growth. Despite this growth, the company lacked a product function and its technology requirements were outsourced to an external engineering team. As a result, there was a disconnect between the co-founders' ambition and the technical roadmap.

Within a month of joining the company, I raised my concerns with the co-founders and was moved into a hybrid product management / product designer role to bring data-driven, commercial-minded, decision-making into the organisation. After conducting an audit of operational processes and speaking with senior management, I identified a lack of accurate reporting and adherence to Key Performance Indicators (KPIs) across the business.

Drawing on my previous experience as a product developer in the fashion industry and my technical savvy gained through a software development bootcamp, I set out to create a suite of reporting products to serve the organisation's ambitious growth plans. I collaborated with the engineering team to develop a suite of reporting products, including a dashboard that displayed real-time transaction data and KPIs. To ensure accuracy, I introduced automated data verification and validation processes that reduced the time and effort required for manual reconciliation. I also implemented a user-friendly interface that made it easy for non-technical staff to access and interpret the data.

The suite of reporting products transformed the way The GoodBox Co operated. The dashboards provided instant access to data and KPIs, empowering staff to make informed decisions. The automated data verification and validation processes eliminated the need for manual reconciliation, freeing up staff time to focus on more strategic initiatives. The user-friendly interface made it easy for staff to access and interpret the data, resulting in increased engagement with the reporting tools. The suite of reporting products ultimately contributed to the growth of the company by providing key insights into its operations and identifying areas for improvement.

Internal Reporting


As the only source of recurring revenue within the organisation, transaction fees were key to measuring the health of the business - as such a regular and accurate transaction report was key to success. Upon joining The GoodBox Co, I discovered that the existing transaction report was in the form of an Excel spreadsheet which was difficult to read and, as it turned out, inaccurate.

I took on the task of creating an accessible and accurate transaction report that would serve as a reliable measure of the business's recurring revenue and health. I worked through a number of iterations, gathering feedback each week from internal stakeholders, and gradually increasing the fidelity of the report. I created a live dashboard using SQL queries and PowerBI, which included tabs with a report for different periods of time (all time, quarterly, monthly, and weekly).

Creating an accessible and accurate transaction report enabled me to demonstrate that the business was missing its defining KPI [donations per device per day] by a factor of 10. This led to a number of strategic pivots in the business model and projected breakeven point, including a shift to upsell hardware, introducing a monthly service charge, and developing a professional services package. As a result, the company experienced a 30-40x increase in monthly recurring revenue.

From this initial reporting product, I derived significant value for the company. The transaction report enabled leadership to track customer acquisition, number of clients, number of devices, and total donations in order to report on growth to investors. I combined transaction data with open-source charity data to enable sales teams to produce benchmark reports and approach leads with Return on Investment (ROI) projections. I highlighted key highlights for the marketing team to create social content and send personalised emails to clients reaching significant fundraising milestones.

Through my efforts in creating an accessible and accurate transaction report, I was able to provide the company with valuable insights and data-driven decision-making. This led to a significant increase in monthly recurring revenue, strategic pivots in the business model, and the creation of value-added products and services.

External Reporting



As with many startups, The GoodBox Co faced significant resource challenges as it tried to develop, prototype, and build secure contactless payment devices without huge investment. As the lead on New Product Development (NPD), my challenge was to generate increased recurring revenue through product initiatives despite the limited budget.

Building on the transaction report that I had developed for internal stakeholder use, I proposed the development of a professional services package for enterprise clients. This package would include custom reporting dashboards with key insights highlighted. To create this package, I conducted a UX audit and analytics assessment on our customer-facing transaction reporting portal. Working closely with our Customer Success manager, I conducted a significant number of user discovery interviews to identify shortcomings in our reporting portal. Based on the findings, I proposed the development of a Minimum Viable Product (MVP) built using SQL queries and PowerBI.

The MVP allowed us to launch the professional services package for enterprise clients, enabling us to generate increased recurring revenue without any significant budget. The custom reporting dashboards with key insights highlighted were a hit among enterprise clients, leading to increased engagement and satisfaction. Additionally, the MVP and the insights generated from user discovery interviews were leveraged to further improve the customer-facing transaction reporting portal, enhancing the overall user experience.

Key features of the reporting dashboard included:

Performance vs. target across previous three-months

This section provides a quick overview of the fundraising performance across the previous three months vs. fundraising the goal. The gauge is utilised in this instance to provide a familiar, user friendly visualisation which is very well suited to displaying performance against a pre-defined goal or KPI.

Dynamic badges are also displayed for each gauge, in this instance communicating the number of transactions per device per day with a colour automatically applied within the dashboard to indicate if this KPI was achieved.


Fundraising milestones achieved with The GoodBox Co

The milestone section is designed to give a visual representation on overall fundraising success using target metrics. The icons were chosen specifically to evoke a journey towards takeoff. The colour is dynamically updated as and when milestones are reached - gradually resulting in less of the psychologically challenging red colour within the interface - and each milestone is customisable per client.


Status overview for device usage

In the status section, fundraisers can see an overview of the number of devices they own plus how long they have been fundraising with The GoodBox Co. Crucially, they can also see success measures for both devices and donations.

The active devices metric tallies the devices owned by the fundraiser which have taken a donation in the previous week. The donations per week metric is a count of total donations through fundraising devices. As is consistent across the dashboard, the fundraiser can set the success criteria for these measures and the badge colour will update accordingly.


Monthly fundraising snapshot by day

The grouped bar chart is a visualisation which most people have knowledge of, it is also one of the most readable charts for data comparison. In this dashboard, the fundraiser can quickly read performance vs. the previous month at a granular level. As this chart develops across the month, the fundraiser can measure performance at any point during the month and adjust fundraising goals or forecasts accordingly.


Outcome

For a small investment per client, I was able to design and build a custom weekly dashboard with key fundraising insights and highlights, resulting in a projected 15x increase in Customer Lifetime Value (CLV). Additionally, the work completed formed the basis of the software product roadmap - the validation collected allowed the company to invest efficiently in full feature development of the transaction reporting portal.
