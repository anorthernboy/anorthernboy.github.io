The GoodBox Co transac tions reporting suite

Introducing data-driven, commercial-minded, decision-making into a rapidly scaling startup

Overview

The GoodBox Co is a tech-for-good startup in the FinTech space, helping charities and nonprofits transition to a cashless society through intuitive digital fundraising tools. I joined the company in 2019 as a Frontend Developer / UI Designer and found an organization experiencing massive growth while trying to serve everyone from enterprise clients through to small community churches.

Within a month of joining the company I raised my concerns with the co-founders around the lack of a product function within the business. The co-founders' backgrounds were in finance and marketing, and their technology requirements were primarily outsourced to an external engineering team - which had created some disconnect between the founders' ambition and the technical roadmap. I was quickly moved into a product management role with the goal of bringing data-driven, commercial-minded, decision-making into the organization.

After speaking to those in senior management roles - and conducting an audit of operational processes - it became apparent that there was a lack of accurate reporting and adherence to Key Performance Indicators (KPIs) across the business.

Leaning heavily on my previous experience as a product developer in the fashion industry, my technical savvy gained through a software development bootcamp, and my natural inclination toward clean, modern, visual design I set out to create a suite of reporting products which could serve the organization in its ambitious growth plans.

Internal Reporting


As the only source of recurring revenue within the organisation, transaction fees were key to measuring the health of the business - as such a regular and accurate transaction report was key to success. When I joined the company, this transaction report took the form of an Excel spreadsheet which was difficult to read (and, as it turned out, inaccurate).

Working through a number of iterations, gathering feedback each week, and gradually increasing the fidelity I created a live dashboard using SQL queries and PowerBI. The transaction report included tabs with a report for different periods of time (all time, quarterly, monthly, and weekly).

In creating an accessible and accurate transaction report I was able to demonstrate that the business was missing its defining KPI [donations per device per day] - one which had been used to project financials to investors - by a factor of 10.

This directly resulted in a number of strategic pivots in the business model and projected breakeven point. We implemented a shift to upsell hardware, introduced a monthly service charge, and started to develop a professional services package - all of which resulted in a 30-40x increase in monthly recurring revenue.

From this initial reporting product, I was able to derive a huge amount of value:

- I enabled leadership to track customer acquisition, number of clients, number of devices, and total donations in order to report on growth to investors
- In combining transaction data with open-source charity data, I enabled sales teams to produce benchmark reports and approach leads with Return on Investment (ROI) projections
- I highlighted key highlights for the marketing team to create social content and send personalised emails to clients reaching significant fundraising milestones

External Reporting


As with many startups, The GoodBox Co faced huge resource challenges. Developing, prototyping, and building hardware in the form of secure contactless payment devices accounted for a huge amount of every budget. The challenge I faced in New Product Development (NPD) was to generate increased recurring revenue through product initiatives - without any significant budget.

Building on the transaction report developed for internal stakeholder use, I proposed the development of a professional services package for enterprise clients which included custom reporting dashboards with key insights highlighted. This proposal was driven by completing a UX audit and analytics assessment on our customer-facing transaction reporting portal.

Working closely with our Customer Success manager, I conducted a significant number of user discovery interviews to identify shortcomings in our reporting portal and proposed the development of a Minimum Viable Product (MVP) built using SQL queries and PowerBI.

Key Takeaways

For a small investment per client, I was able to design and build a custom weekly dashboard with key fundraising insights and highlights, resulting in a projected 15x increase in Customer Lifetime Value (CLV). Additionally, the work completed formed the basis of the software product roadmap - the validation collected allowed the company to invest efficiently in full feature development of the transaction reporting portal.
